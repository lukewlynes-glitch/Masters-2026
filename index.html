Key Statistical Insights

SG: Approach is King

5 of the last 7 winners ranked top 5 in SG: Approach during tournament week. The average Masters winner gains +5.8 strokes on approach vs. the field over 4 rounds.

Par 5s = Separation

Par 5 scoring accounts for 73-88% of the winner's total advantage. Average champion is 8-under on par 5s across 4 rounds. All 4 par 5s are reachable in two for long hitters.

Hot Form is Non-Negotiable

13 consecutive winners gained 18+ strokes tee-to-green in their 4 events prior to the Masters. Scheffler gained +47.2 before his 2024 win — the highest in the dataset.

Course Experience Matters More Here

No first-time Augusta visitor has won since Fuzzy Zoeller in 1979. Course history is more predictive at Augusta than any other venue in professional golf.

Augusta National — Course DNA

Par 72 — 7,565 yards — Bentgrass greens — 180 feet of elevation change. The course rewards elite iron play, length off the tee, and imagination around the greens. Driving accuracy is less important here than at most venues — fairways are wide, but distance to reach par 5s in two is critical.

Hole 11 — White Dogwood (520 yds, Par 4)

The hardest par 4 on the course. Avg scoring: 4.30. Pond left guards the green. The 2nd shot is the most demanding approach at Augusta.

Hole 12 — Golden Bell (155 yds, Par 3)

The most famous par 3 in golf. Avg scoring: 3.09. Swirling winds through the trees make club selection a guessing game. Rae's Creek awaits short.

Hole 13 — Azalea (545 yds, Par 5)

Risk-reward par 5, easiest hole on the course. Avg scoring: 4.78. Reachable in two for anyone in the field. Eagles happen here regularly on Sunday.
